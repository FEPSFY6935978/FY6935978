Abstract

    The purpose of this paper will be to find an average time to complete the Omega Ruby/Alpha Sapphire Regional
    Pokédex and Shiny Regional Pokédex. The average time will be calculated using a combination of personal 
    gameplay, calculated encounter times and catch rates. The average time I will be calculate may not be entirely 
    accurate, and relies on luck with encounters and averages on a large scale, mainly for the Shiny Pokédex 
    where trying to actually complete it would take an extremely long time as the odds of encountering a shiny
    pokémon are very low. The final time I got was 36 hours and 23 minutes for the Regional Pokédex, and an estimate
    of 2417 hours and 46 minutes for the Shiny Regional Pokédex.

Exclusions

    For both versions of the regional pokédex, I will be excluding Jirachi as it is a mythical Pokémon, meaning it
    can only be obtained from events which are no longer running, making it unobtainable. The excluded Pokémon for
    the shiny regional pokédex are Kyogre, Groudon, Rayquaza and Deoxys, as these 4 Pokémon are shiny-locked in
    Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire, meaning their shiny forms cannot be obtained.

Regional Pokédex

    The average time to complete the Generation 6 Hoenn Regional Pokédex will be determined by using the
    playthrough times for the multiple playthroughs of Alpha Sapphire and Omega Ruby necessary to complete
    the Regional Pokédex. For the playthroughs, I will be using a Nintendo 2DS, a physical cartridge of Pokémon
    Alpha Sapphire, and a physical cartridge of Pokémon Omega Ruby, with all the equipment previously mentioned
    being unmodified. The time I obtained I will consider as the average time and will be the base time I will
    use for the Shiny Regional Pokédex. The time it took me to complete the Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire
    Regional Pokédex was 36 hours, 23 minutes.
